about

I am an assistant professor at Yale-NUS College in Singapore and also associate researcher with the Climate Futures Initiative at the Princeton Environmental Institute. I have been a postdoctoral research fellow at the Woodrow Wilson School and the University Center for Human Values at Princeton University and, more recently, a fellow at the Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences at Stanford University.

I am interested in economic policy questions relating to climate change and inequality. How we evaluate climate change economically, will depend importantly on how unequal the societies are that will be exposed to climate change, and how the negative effects of climate change are distributed across groups in those societies. For example, it is possible that climate change as well as climate policy will affect poor people disproportionately. Climate policy might thereby have an important impact on inequality. This aspect of climate policy is often overlooked in policy evaluation studies. I am also interested in understanding other determinants of economic inequality. With another group of colleagues I am investigating other long term determinants of inequality. How does family planning affect the distribution of capital in the long run; how does the market structure in the supply of education affect the distribution and the returns to human capital. These are natural questions to tackle with the toolkit of economics.
