I am an assistant professor at Yale-NUS College in Singapore - currently on leave at the World Bank - and an associate researcher with the Climate Futures Initiative at the Princeton Environmental Institute.
I work primarily on climate change policy, where my academic research focuses on global policy trade-offs, while my current policy oriented work at the World Bank focuses specifically on the trade-offs confronted by developing countries in dealing with the implications of climate change and the implementation of climate policy. In addition to climate economics I have ongoing research on inequality and population economics, focusing specifically on the impact of intergenerational transmission on the dynamics of the income distribution.
